## 4Ô∏è‚É£ Growth Strategy ‚Äî Building a "Network of Necessity"

TexQtic grows by becoming **mandatory infrastructure, not optional software**.

### A. Top-Down Regulatory Pull (Brands ‚Üí Suppliers)

By 2027, the **EU Digital Product Passport (DPP)** will be mandatory for textiles.

**Strategy:**

- Position TexQtic as the **Compliance Interface for Brands**
- Brands use TexQtic to manage DPP obligations
- Brands mandate Tier-1 to Tier-4 suppliers to participate by emitting **Verified Certification Events**

**Outcome:**

- One brand legally pulls hundreds of suppliers into the platform
- Adoption is driven by the supplier's **"Right to Sell"** in regulated markets

### B. Bottom-Up Supplier Workflow Push (Administrative Debt)

Suppliers lose significant margin to:

- manual certificate management
- forged documentation
- repeated audit prep

**Strategy:**

- Solve supplier administrative debt first
- Automate certificate management
- Enable batch-level traceability
- Reduce audit friction

**Outcome:**

- Suppliers join because TexQtic saves time and reduces risk
- Engagement precedes monetization
- Trust forms before demand aggregation

### C. Cold-Start Resolution via Human Curation

TexQtic does not rely on algorithms to bootstrap trust.

**Strategy:**

- Manual matchmaking for early transactions
- Human-verified trades (first ~500)
- No-fee core discovery for small producers

**Outcome:**

- Trust forms faster than algorithmic marketplaces
- Early events become high-quality training data
- Reputation is earned, not inferred
