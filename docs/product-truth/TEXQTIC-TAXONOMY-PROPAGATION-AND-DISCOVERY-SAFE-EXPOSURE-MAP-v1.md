## 1. Purpose and Authority

This artifact records one bounded taxonomy propagation and discovery-safe exposure map for TexQtic.

Its purpose is narrow:

- fix where the full B2B textile taxonomy is owned versus where it is only carried, consumed, or
  exposed in bounded form
- define the stage-by-stage propagation rule for taxonomy signals from recommendation language into
  lawful persisted runtime truth
- define the surface-by-surface visibility boundary for common tenant-admin, B2B-depth, Aggregator,
  WL, and platform-supervisory contexts
- prevent the Aggregator directory/profile subfamily from being misread as full taxonomy ownership
  transfer

This artifact does not:

- modify Layer 0 governance posture
- reopen the locked architecture statement
- authorize runtime, route, schema, or contract mutation
- implement taxonomy, discovery, provisioning, tenant-admin, or navigation behavior
- define taxonomy tables, enums, or persistence schema
- replace the controlling vocabulary/state-map artifact, provisioning alignment matrix, or
  tenant-admin surface matrix

Usage rule:

- later bounded planning artifacts and implementation-facing planning must inherit this map only
  together with the controlling vocabulary/state-map artifact, the provisioning alignment matrix,
  and the tenant-admin surface matrix
- this artifact governs taxonomy propagation, bounded exposure, and non-transfer interpretation only;

## 3. Inherited Controlling Truth

This map inherits, without reinterpretation, all of the following:

- base commercial families remain `B2B` and `B2C`
- `INTERNAL` remains a non-commercial category
- `WHITE_LABEL` remains an overlay/capability, not a peer family
- `AGGREGATOR` remains a cross-family capability and bounded runtime/workspace context, not a peer
  family
- provisioning/runtime remains the first canonical persisted owner of family/package truth
- tenant-admin remains one common core with bounded overlays rather than separate whole offices
- B2B owns the full internal textile taxonomy, including primary segment, secondary segments, and
  role-position axis truth
- Aggregator may consume only the discovery-safe subset of that B2B taxonomy for discovery,
  qualification, inspection, and handoff
- consuming a subset never transfers full taxonomy administration, execution ownership, or parent
  family truth to Aggregator
- the Aggregator directory/profile subfamily remains a bounded discovery/handoff placement rather
  than a transfer of the full B2B taxonomy into Aggregator ownership

## 4. Taxonomy Drift This Map Resolves

Current repo/product-truth drift requiring this map is:

- runtime identity labels and shell context can make `AGGREGATOR` look like a full taxonomy owner
  rather than a bounded subset consumer
- provisioning and runtime records can be misread as the semantic owner of taxonomy truth rather
  than the first lawful persisted carrier of already-governed taxonomy assignment
- common tenant-admin and WL surfaces can over-expose B2B taxonomy depth if no bounded visibility
  rule is fixed
- the Aggregator directory/profile subfamily can be misread as if discovery profile placement moves
  the entire supplier/manufacturer taxonomy into Aggregator ownership
- descriptive dashboard surfaces still overstate supplier network, buyer network, and directory depth
  in ways that can blur discovery-safe subset exposure with full B2B taxonomy administration

## 7. Propagation and Non-Transfer Rules

- Full semantic ownership of the textile taxonomy remains with the B2B family even when provisioning
  and runtime records carry canonical taxonomy fields.
- Provisioning/runtime may persist and surface B2B taxonomy truth, but persistence does not make
  provisioning/runtime the semantic owner of taxonomy meaning.
- Common tenant-admin may expose only bounded internal summaries derived from canonical persisted
  truth; it must not become a full taxonomy workbench.
- Aggregator may consume only the discovery-safe subset and only for discovery, qualification,
  inspection, and handoff.
- The Supplier / Manufacturer Capability Directory / Profiling placement under Aggregator is a
  discovery-surface placement only; it does not move full taxonomy administration or execution
  ownership out of B2B.
- Once a user leaves Aggregator and enters shared downstream continuation, the receiving family or
  surface owns the next lawful taxonomy visibility rule; Aggregator ownership ends at handoff.
- WHITE_LABEL may inherit bounded display of parent-family taxonomy truth, but it may not redefine
  taxonomy axes or create a parallel taxonomy model.

## 8. Allowed and Disallowed Planning-Level Interpretations

Allowed interpretation patterns:

- B2B may own the full taxonomy while provisioning/runtime persist that truth as a separate carrier
  axis.
- common tenant-admin may display bounded taxonomy summaries without becoming taxonomy owner.
- Aggregator may display discovery-safe subset values derived from B2B-owned truth without gaining
  full administrative or execution ownership.
- downstream B2B continuity may lawfully consume fuller taxonomy depth after Aggregator handoff so
  long as the surface is classified as downstream re-entry rather than Aggregator-owned depth.
- WL surfaces may inherit bounded display of parent-family taxonomy truth without becoming a new
  taxonomy authority.

Disallowed interpretation patterns:

- treating `AGGREGATOR` runtime identity, shell presence, or directory placement as proof that
  Aggregator owns the full B2B taxonomy
- treating provisioning or runtime persistence as if it defines the semantic taxonomy model rather
  than carrying B2B-owned truth
- exposing full internal taxonomy depth through common tenant-admin, WL-admin, or generic directory
  surfaces when only bounded summary or discovery-safe subset visibility is lawful
- treating discovery-safe subset exposure as if it transfers negotiation, execution, or full
  business-participant classification ownership to Aggregator
- treating shared downstream continuation reached from Aggregator context as Aggregator-owned
  taxonomy depth

## 9. Blockers and Edge Conditions Inside The Map Boundary

- descriptive tenant dashboard and shell-era surfaces still use directory, supplier-network, and
  buyer-network language loosely, so this map must be treated as the stronger taxonomy interpretation
  reference until later bounded reconciliation updates those surfaces

  those labels do not override B2B taxonomy ownership or the subset boundary defined here
- White Label Co remains a preserved Layer 0 hold, but that hold does not block this artifact
