## 1. Purpose and Authority

`Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility`.

Its purpose is narrow:

- define the lawful downstream finance-state family under the adopted canonical model
- separate downstream finance-state continuity from parent commercial access model ownership,

  commercial packaging truth, and generic finance/ledger shell presence

  not keep inferring payments-family truth from B2B trade language, order progression, control-
  plane finance surfaces, or subscription billing wording alone

This artifact does not authorize implementation, does not redesign adjacent families, and does not
replace Layer 0 governance truth.

## 2. Current Controlling Posture

Current repo authority already fixes the following truths:

- TexQtic is the operating system for trusted textile supply chains.
- The only governed commercial access models are B2B Exchange, B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce, and
  Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff.
- White-label is an overlay capability and deployment/experience model, not a separate commercial
  access model.
- Enterprise is subordinate depth within B2B, not a separate platform mode.
- Control-plane, superadmin, and WL-admin surfaces are governance/operations surfaces, not
  commercial pillars.
- Current doctrine and status truth already fix the `not fintech now` posture: direct tenant-to-
  tenant settlement now, platform as workflow system-of-record, and no platform-held funds.
- Payments, escrow, settlement, fee visibility, and finance-event supervision are materially
  evidenced across multiple adjacent planning and runtime surfaces now, but their family-level
  planning authority remains fragmented.

The remaining planning gap is not whether downstream finance-state continuity exists. The gap is
that current repo truth still describes it through neighboring families and evidence surfaces:

- B2B uses escrow and settlement visibility as part of governed exchange continuity.
- Orders explicitly defer payment-state and settlement-state continuity to a separate later family.
- platform control-plane records finance oversight, settlement-event visibility, and fee visibility
  at platform scope without thereby owning the whole downstream family.
- subscriptions/commercial packaging work records billing and plan-language drift that is adjacent
  to finance-state wording but not identical to transaction finance-state truth.
- strategy-era dashboard and control-center artifacts preserve finance-domain and finance-tower
  language that is useful evidence, but easy to misread as family authority.

Those are all real evidence surfaces. None of them alone is the canonical whole-family definition.

## 3. Lawful Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility Classification

The controlling classification is:

- payments / escrow / settlement / fee visibility is a distinct downstream finance-state family
- this family is not a governed commercial access model
- this family is not identical to B2B exchange, B2C storefront continuity, Aggregator handoff, or
  downstream orders-family ownership
- this family is not identical to platform control-plane finance oversight, subscription /
  commercial packaging, white-label overlay, tenant back-office administration, or catalog /
  discovery ownership
- this family is bounded by current `system-of-record, not funds` posture and must not be defined
  as platform money movement, custody, or activated PSP execution

Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility therefore exists as the downstream finance-state
family that carries lawful parent and downstream order contexts into payment acknowledgement,
escrow-state visibility, settlement-state visibility, fee visibility, and bounded finance-event
progression without replacing the parent or downstream family that led into it.

## 4. What Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility Means In TexQtic Planning Truth

Within TexQtic planning truth, `Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility` means the shared
downstream family of finance-state continuity by which lawful tenant and platform participants can
observe, acknowledge, and progress payment-adjacent events without turning TexQtic into a funds-
moving platform.

At the family level, this means:

- payment-state continuity where repo truth supports acknowledgement, status, or event visibility
  rather than platform-held money movement
- escrow-state continuity or escrow visibility where repo truth supports governed transaction-state
  inspection without claiming mature custody behavior
- settlement acknowledgement, settlement-event visibility, and settlement-state continuity where
  repo truth supports them
- transaction-fee visibility and fee-event continuity where repo truth supports platform-earned fee
  or order-attached fee visibility
- bounded finance-event progression that can be reused across lawful parent contexts without
  redefining those parent contexts or the downstream orders family

The correct high-level family statement is:

- Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility is the downstream finance-state family that
  preserves truthful payment acknowledgement, escrow visibility, settlement visibility, and fee
  visibility across lawful parent and downstream order contexts without replacing exchange,
  storefront, order, control-plane, or subscription family ownership.

## 5. What Belongs Inside Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility Scope

The following belong inside the downstream finance-state family boundary:

### 5.1 Payment-state continuity

- payment acknowledgement or payment-event visibility where repo truth supports them
- payment-state read continuity attached to a lawful transaction or order context
- bounded payment-status progression that remains system-of-record-only under current doctrine

### 5.2 Escrow-state and escrow-visibility continuity

- escrow-state visibility or escrow read continuity where repo truth already supports it
- escrow-event continuity as governed transaction-state visibility rather than mature funds custody
- bounded escrow-related transaction state that remains within current no-funds platform posture

### 5.3 Settlement acknowledgement and settlement-state continuity

- settlement acknowledgement records
- settlement-event visibility and settlement-state continuity
- bounded settlement progression attached to transaction workflow where repo truth supports it

### 5.4 Fee visibility and fee-event continuity

- platform fee visibility attached to orders, transactions, or platform-earned value where repo
  truth supports it
- fee-event continuity and transaction-fee recording visibility
- bounded reconciliation-facing finance events where the purpose is finance-state truth rather than
  a broad finance-ops redesign

### 5.5 System-of-record posture as a family boundary

- explicit distinction between finance-state recording and money movement
- direct tenant-to-tenant settlement posture under current doctrine
- architecture-hook awareness where PSP/bank integration remains unactivated and outside current
  runtime ownership

### 5.6 Shared downstream family reuse across parent contexts

- downstream finance-state continuity reused by B2B without collapsing the whole family into B2B
- downstream finance-state continuity connected from orders/checkout without making orders the owner
  of payment, escrow, or settlement truth in full
- B2C- or WL-adjacent finance-state surfaces where those surfaces expose downstream finance-state
  continuity rather than define the parent or overlay family

## 6. What Does Not Belong Inside Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility Scope

The following are adjacent or later families and must not be silently absorbed into downstream
finance-state design:

### 6.1 Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase as a whole family

- cart, checkout, order creation, order visibility, and bounded post-order execution continuity as
  a whole-family substitute
- any attempt to define finance-state continuity through order-family ownership alone

### 6.2 B2B Exchange Core as a whole family

- governed business exchange posture, RFQ, negotiation, trade, compliance, and commercial entry as
  the whole B2B family
- authenticated business exchange context treated as if it defines the whole downstream finance
  family
- enterprise depth classification

### 6.3 B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce as a whole family

- public-safe discovery, branded storefront entry, and consumer parent-family truth as a substitute
  for the downstream finance family
- consumer storefront or browse-entry language treated as if it defines payment-state truth in full

### 6.4 Platform Control-Plane as a whole family

- platform finance oversight, tenant billing supervision, disputes, audit, and global operator
  control as a whole-family substitute
- platform-level finance tower or admin shell presence treated as if it defines the downstream
  finance family itself

### 6.5 Subscriptions / Commercial Packaging / Entitlements as a whole family

- plan identity, entitlement enforcement, billing-status drift, commercial packaging, and
  subscription lifecycle as a whole-family substitute
- any attempt to define transaction finance-state continuity through plan or billing vocabulary
  alone

### 6.6 Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity as a whole family

- product-data, browse, list, detail, or discovery continuity as a whole-family substitute for
  finance-state ownership
- commercial discovery or merchandising depth treated as if it proves finance-state completeness

### 6.7 White-Label Overlay and Tenant Back Office as whole families

- branded runtime presentation, WL operator framing, or overlay deployment behavior as a whole-
  family substitute
- memberships, branding administration, settings, and tenant-owned workspace controls as a
  substitute for finance-state family scope

### 6.8 Identity, messaging, domain, compliance-depth, or implementation families

- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace family ownership
- messaging / notifications family ownership
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management family ownership
- broad compliance, audit-export, reconciliation-platform, or finance-ops redesign beyond bounded
  current finance-state truth
- implementation planning, PSP activation, funds-movement redesign, schema changes, or runtime
  completion claims

## 7. Relationship To Adjacent Families

### 7.1 Relationship to Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase

- Orders and checkout may lead into downstream finance-state continuity.
- The finance-state family begins where order execution needs payment, escrow, settlement, or fee
  status continuity beyond the order-family boundary.
- Orders does not define the whole finance-state family.

### 7.2 Relationship to B2B Exchange Core

- B2B uses payment, escrow, and settlement visibility as part of governed exchange continuity.
- The downstream finance-state family supplies finance-state continuity without replacing B2B as
  the parent commercial access model.
- B2B-specific exchange posture remains separate from the shared finance-state boundary defined
  here.

### 7.3 Relationship to B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce

- B2C may connect into downstream finance-state continuity through lawful downstream paths.
- The finance-state family does not define B2C parent-family storefront entry, public-safe
  discovery, or branded consumer posture.
- B2C and downstream finance-state continuity must remain separate so parent-family truth is not
  mistaken for finance-state ownership.

### 7.4 Relationship to Platform Control-Plane

- Platform control-plane may supervise fee visibility, settlement-event visibility, billing-status
  supervision, and related finance oversight at platform scope.
- That supervision does not define the downstream finance-state family itself.
- Platform finance surfaces are evidence of bounded oversight, not canonical ownership of all
  finance-state continuity.

### 7.5 Relationship to Subscriptions / Commercial Packaging / Entitlements

- Subscription family truth covers plan identity, commercial packaging, and bounded entitlement
  enforcement.
- Subscription billing wording must not be used as a substitute for transaction payment-state,
  escrow-state, settlement-state, or fee-event continuity.
- The finance-state family remains distinct from commercial packaging and entitlement truth.

### 7.6 Relationship to Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity

- Catalog/discovery may supply the items, counterparties, or commercial context that eventually
  feed downstream finance-state events.
- Catalog truth does not define payment, escrow, settlement, or fee visibility ownership.

### 7.7 Relationship to White-Label Overlay

- White-label may affect how downstream finance-state surfaces are presented or reached where
  lawful.
- White-label does not define the whole finance-state family and does not replace it as a cross-
  mode downstream boundary.

### 7.8 Relationship to Tenant Back Office

- Tenant back office may expose tenant-owned operator access to finance-state surfaces in bounded
  form.
- Tenant-admin controls do not define the downstream finance-state family.

### 7.9 Relationship to Identity / Tenancy / Permissions / Workspace Continuity

- finance-state continuity depends on lawful tenant, user, and workspace context.
- that dependency does not authorize absorbing the full identity/workspace family here.

### 7.10 Relationship to Messaging / Notifications

- finance-state events may later generate or require messaging/notification behavior.
- the full messaging / notifications family remains separate and must not be silently merged into
  finance-state ownership.

### 7.11 Relationship to Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management

- domain or brand-surface concerns may affect how finance-state surfaces are reached.
- that overlap does not authorize solving the full domain/routing/brand-surface family here.

## 8. Current Sources Of Drift

The main current drift classes are:

- B2B exchange language already includes escrow and settlement visibility, which can make the
  parent exchange family look like the owner of the whole finance-state boundary
- orders-family truth explicitly borders payments and settlement, which can make downstream
  finance-state continuity look like a mere extension of order progression
- platform control-plane finance wording and control-center taxonomy preserve real platform finance
  oversight but can be misread as whole-family ownership
- subscription normalization and implementation-design artifacts preserve billing-language drift and
  commercial-packaging concerns that are adjacent to, but not identical with, transaction
  finance-state continuity
- tenant dashboard matrix finance domains and generic finance/escrow/settlement shell labels can be
  mistaken for lawful family authority even where they are largely planned, stubbed, or descriptive
- status and doctrine wording about transaction fees, direct tenant-to-tenant settlement, and
  future hookpoints can be over-read into claims of activated PSP, funds custody, or platform-
  managed settlement ownership

These sources remain useful evidence of runtime or bounded planning truth, but they must not serve
as the canonical family-classification authority going forward.

## 9. Inheritance Rules For Later Family Units

Later family-specific units must inherit all of the following:

1. Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility is a distinct downstream finance-state family.
2. This family is not itself a parent commercial access model.
3. Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase may lead into this family, but do not define the whole family.
4. B2B, B2C, and WL-adjacent contexts may connect into this family without becoming its canonical
   owner.
5. Platform control-plane finance oversight remains separate from tenant/transaction-level
   finance-state continuity.
6. Subscription / commercial packaging / entitlements remain separate later family work.
7. Current repo truth supports system-of-record finance-state continuity, not platform money
   movement, funds custody, or activated PSP ownership.
8. Generic finance tower presence, one ledger widget, one settlement admin panel, or one dashboard
   domain must not be used as proof of the whole family definition.
9. This family boundary must not be overstated into mature billing-platform, reconciliation-platform,
   or fintech-product completeness.
10. This family boundary does not imply implementation completeness.

## 10. Deferred To Later Units

This family anchor deliberately does not solve:

- subscription billing lifecycle or broader commercial packaging redesign
- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace family design
- messaging / notifications family design
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management family design
- broader finance-ops, reconciliation-platform, or audit-export family design
- PSP activation, bank-hook activation, milestone escrow activation, or platform-managed settlement
- implementation planning, runtime changes, or defect remediation

Those remain later bounded units.
