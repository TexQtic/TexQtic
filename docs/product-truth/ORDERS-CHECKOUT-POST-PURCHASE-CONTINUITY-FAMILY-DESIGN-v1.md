`Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase Continuity`.

- define the lawful downstream transaction-continuity family under the adopted canonical model

## 2. Current Controlling Posture

Current repo authority already fixes the following truths:

- TexQtic is the operating system for trusted textile supply chains.
- The only governed commercial access models are B2B Exchange, B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce, and
  Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff.
- White-label is an overlay capability and deployment/experience model, not a separate commercial
  access model.
- Enterprise is subordinate depth within B2B, not a separate platform mode.
- Control-plane, superadmin, and WL-admin surfaces are governance/operations surfaces, not
  commercial pillars.
- Cart, checkout, orders, and bounded post-order progression are materially evidenced in multiple
  runtime and planning surfaces now, but their family-level planning authority remains fragmented.

The remaining planning gap is not whether downstream transaction continuity exists. The gap is
that current repo truth still describes it through neighboring families and evidence surfaces:

- B2B uses cart, checkout, and order continuity as part of the authenticated exchange loop.
- B2C explicitly points to cart, checkout, order, and post-purchase as a later downstream family
  rather than parent-family truth.
- white-label exposes storefront/cart continuity and WL operator order visibility without thereby
  becoming the owner of the whole downstream family.
- launch-readiness truth records B2B commerce core and narrow fulfillment/post-order evidence, but
  does not classify the whole family.
- current-state truth records a live checkout defect and cart-response mismatch, but those runtime
  defects are not lawful substitutes for family classification.

Those are all real evidence surfaces. None of them alone is the canonical whole-family definition.

## 3. Lawful Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase Classification

The controlling classification is:

- orders / checkout / post-purchase continuity is a cross-mode downstream transaction-continuity
  family
- this family is not a governed commercial access model
- this family is not identical to B2B exchange, B2C storefront continuity, or Aggregator
  discovery-and-handoff ownership
- this family is not identical to catalog/discovery/product-data ownership, white-label overlay,
  tenant back-office administration, or platform control-plane supervision
- this family is adjacent to payments / escrow / settlement, but is not defined by funds movement,
  fee logic, settlement governance, or finance oversight

Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase Continuity therefore exists as the downstream commerce-execution
family that carries lawful parent contexts from item selection into checkout, order formation,
order visibility, and bounded post-order progression without replacing the parent family that led
into it.

## 4. What Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase Means In TexQtic Planning Truth

Within TexQtic planning truth, `Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase Continuity` means the shared
downstream family of cart state, checkout initiation, order creation/read continuity, and bounded
after-order progression that can be reused by lawful parent contexts without redefining those
parent contexts.

At the family level, this means:

- cart continuity that preserves selected item state as a real transition toward execution rather
  than as decorative shell presence alone
- checkout continuity that captures the bounded transition from selected commercial items into an
  order-intent or order-creation step
- order continuity that lets a lawful tenant or operator inspect created orders, statuses, and
  related execution state in a materially real way
- bounded post-purchase continuity such as fulfillment-status progression or after-order review
  where repo truth already materially exposes it
- downstream continuity reused across B2B, B2C-adjacent, and WL-adjacent contexts without changing
  which parent family owns the commercial entry posture

The correct high-level family statement is:

- Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase Continuity is the cross-mode downstream transaction family that
  preserves truthful cart-to-order-to-bounded-post-order continuity across lawful parent contexts
  without replacing exchange ownership, storefront ownership, overlay ownership, or payments
  ownership.

## 5. What Belongs Inside Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase Scope

The following belong inside the downstream orders/checkout/post-purchase family boundary:

### 5.1 Cart and pre-order execution continuity

- add-to-cart and cart-state continuity where the purpose is to preserve a real downstream
  execution path rather than merely support browse or merchandising presence
- cart inspection, quantity, line-item, and review continuity where those behaviors are part of the
  downstream execution seam
- bounded cart-to-checkout transition behavior

### 5.2 Checkout continuity

- checkout initiation and submission continuity
- checkout-state validation or transition behavior where it exists to create or advance a lawful
  order step
- authenticated downstream execution continuity after the parent browse/exchange step has already
  occurred

### 5.3 Order creation, visibility, and lifecycle continuity

- created-order visibility and order list/detail continuity
- order status continuity and bounded execution-state progression attached to the order lifecycle
- operator or tenant read surfaces that make downstream order progression materially usable

### 5.4 Bounded post-purchase continuity

- bounded after-order continuity such as fulfillment-state visibility where repo truth already
  supports it
- post-order progression that remains closer to order execution than to finance settlement,
  messaging, or broad shipment/returns redesign
- downstream continuity that may exist across parent contexts even when its exact depth differs by
  mode

### 5.5 Shared downstream family reuse across parent contexts

- downstream order continuity reused by B2B without collapsing the whole family into B2B
- downstream order continuity connected from B2C parent-family entry without making B2C the owner
  of all downstream transaction rules
- WL-adjacent order or operator surfaces where those surfaces expose or manage downstream order
  continuity rather than define the entire overlay family

## 6. What Does Not Belong Inside Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase Scope

The following are adjacent or later families and must not be silently absorbed into downstream
orders-family design:

### 6.1 B2B Exchange Core as a whole family

- governed business exchange posture, RFQ, negotiation, trade, compliance, escrow, and settlement
  as the whole B2B family
- authenticated business-commercial context treated as if it defines the whole downstream family
- enterprise depth classification

### 6.2 B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce as a whole family

- public-safe discovery, branded storefront entry, and consumer parent-family truth as a substitute
  for the downstream family
- B2C browse-entry or decorative storefront affordances treated as if they define checkout/order
  continuity in full

### 6.3 Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity as a whole family

- search, browse, list, detail, collection, preview, or product-data continuity as a whole-family
  substitute for downstream order ownership
- item discovery or merchandising depth treated as if it proves checkout/order family completeness

### 6.4 White-Label Overlay as a whole family

- branded runtime presentation, WL-specific storefront framing, or overlay deployment behavior as a
  whole-family substitute
- WL admin/operator order panels treated as if they define the whole downstream family across the
  platform

### 6.5 Tenant Back Office as a whole family

- memberships, branding administration, settings, and tenant-owned workspace controls as a
  substitute for downstream order-family scope
- generic tenant shell presence or operator controls treated as order-family proof

### 6.6 Platform Control-Plane as a whole family

- cross-tenant supervision, audit, compliance casework, finance oversight, or global operator
  controls
- platform visibility into tenant orders treated as if it defines the downstream family

### 6.7 Payments / Escrow / Settlement as a whole family

- fee logic, payment lifecycle, escrow governance, settlement execution, payout policy, or finance
  oversight as a whole-family substitute
- any attempt to define the downstream order family through money-movement or settlement-state
  ownership alone

### 6.8 Identity, messaging, domain, fulfillment-depth, or implementation families

- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace family ownership
- messaging / notifications family ownership
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management family ownership
- shipment, returns, or fulfillment-depth redesign beyond bounded currently evidenced post-order
  continuity
- implementation planning, runtime fixes, API redesign, schema changes, or defect remediation

## 7. Relationship To Adjacent Families

### 7.1 Relationship to B2B Exchange Core

- B2B uses cart, checkout, and order continuity as part of authenticated business exchange.
- The downstream orders family supplies execution continuity without replacing B2B as the parent
  commercial access model.
- B2B-specific RFQ, negotiation, and trade posture remain separate from the shared downstream
  family boundary defined here.

### 7.2 Relationship to B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce

- B2C may connect into downstream cart, checkout, order, and post-purchase continuity.
- The downstream family does not define B2C parent-family storefront entry, public-safe discovery,
  or branded consumer posture.
- B2C and downstream order continuity must remain separate so browse-entry truth is not mistaken
  for transaction-family truth and vice versa.

### 7.3 Relationship to Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity

- Catalog/discovery may supply the inspected items or data that feed downstream execution.
- The downstream family begins where real item selection transitions into execution continuity.
- Catalog truth must not be used as a substitute for proving checkout, order, or post-order
  continuity.

### 7.4 Relationship to White-Label Overlay

- White-label may overlay surfaces that expose cart, checkout, or order continuity where lawful.
- White-label does not define the whole downstream family and does not replace it as a cross-mode
  execution boundary.
- WL operator order visibility is evidence of adjacent reuse, not canonical family ownership.

### 7.5 Relationship to Tenant Back Office

- Tenant back office may support tenant-owned operator access to downstream order surfaces.
- Tenant-admin controls do not define the downstream orders family.
- Order continuity must not be reduced to generic admin-shell presence.

### 7.6 Relationship to Platform Control-Plane

- Platform control-plane may supervise or review downstream order-adjacent operations in bounded
  form.
- That supervision does not define the downstream family itself.

### 7.7 Relationship to Payments / Escrow / Settlement

- Orders and checkout may connect to finance-state or settlement-state continuity later.
- Payments / escrow / settlement remain a separate downstream family and must not be silently
  merged into order-family truth.
- The current downstream family stops short of claiming money-movement ownership.

### 7.8 Relationship to Fulfillment / Shipment / Returns Depth

- bounded fulfillment-state visibility may belong inside this family where it is directly attached
  to post-order continuity already evidenced now
- broader shipment operations, returns loops, and deeper execution workflows remain separately
  refinable later and must not be silently claimed complete here

## 8. Current Sources Of Drift

The main current drift classes are:

- B2B commerce-core strength can make downstream cart, checkout, and order continuity look like it
  belongs wholly to B2B family truth rather than to a reusable downstream family
- B2C storefront language can make downstream transaction continuity look like it is merely a B2C
  extension rather than a separate family
- catalog-backed add-to-cart and browse/product-detail evidence can make selection continuity look
  like sufficient proof of full downstream execution continuity
- WL storefront/cart and WL operator order surfaces can make overlay or WL-admin presence look like
  canonical order-family authority
- launch-readiness and current-state artifacts mix materially real downstream evidence with live
  defects, which can obscure the difference between family ownership and runtime health
- payment, escrow, settlement, fulfillment, or shipment language can over-expand downstream order
  truth into adjacent later families

These sources remain useful evidence of runtime or bounded planning truth, but they must not serve
as the canonical family-classification authority going forward.

## 9. Inheritance Rules For Later Family Units

Later family-specific units must inherit all of the following:

1. Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase Continuity is a distinct downstream cross-mode family.
2. This family is not itself a parent commercial access model.
3. B2B, B2C, and WL-adjacent contexts may connect into this family without becoming its canonical
   owner.
4. Catalog/discovery continuity may feed downstream execution, but does not define the downstream
   order family.
5. Payments / escrow / settlement remain separate later family work.
6. Tenant Back Office and Platform Control-Plane remain separate administrative families.
7. Bounded post-order continuity already evidenced now must not be overstated into full shipment,
   returns, or finance-operations completeness.
8. Generic shell presence, one order panel, or one checkout endpoint must not be used as proof of
   the whole downstream family definition.
9. Runtime defects in cart or checkout do not change the lawful family classification.
10. This family boundary does not imply implementation completeness.

## 10. Deferred To Later Units

- payments / escrow / settlement family design
- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace family design
- messaging / notifications family design
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management family design
- deeper fulfillment / shipment / returns family design beyond bounded current post-order evidence

Those remain later bounded units.
