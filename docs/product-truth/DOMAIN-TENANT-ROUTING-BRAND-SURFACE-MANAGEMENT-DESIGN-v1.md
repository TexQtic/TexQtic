`Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management`.

- define the lawful cross-cutting routing/brand-surface family under the adopted canonical model

  ownership, identity/workspace ownership, tenant back-office ownership, platform control-plane

  keep inferring this family from WL overlay discussions, tenant-resolution seams, control-plane

This artifact does not authorize implementation, does not redesign adjacent families, and does not
replace Layer 0 governance truth.

## 2. Current Controlling Posture

Current repo authority already fixes the following truths:

- TexQtic is the operating system for trusted textile supply chains.
- The only governed commercial access models are B2B Exchange, B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce, and
  Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff.
- White-label is an overlay capability and deployment/experience model, not a separate commercial
  access model.
- Enterprise is subordinate depth within B2B, not a separate platform mode.
- Control-plane, superadmin, and WL-admin surfaces are governance/operations surfaces, not
  commercial pillars.
- Current repo truth already evidences public tenant resolution by slug and by email, realm-safe
  entry posture, branded storefront/admin surfaces, custom-domain management references, and route-
  context-sensitive shell behavior.

The remaining planning gap is not whether routing and brand-surface concerns exist. The gap is
that current repo truth still describes them through neighboring families and evidence surfaces:

- white-label uses branded delivery paths, custom-domain needs, and branded presentation without
  thereby owning the whole cross-cutting family
- identity/workspace uses tenant resolution, realm entry, and workspace context continuity without
  thereby owning all route/brand-surface continuity
- tenant back office uses tenant-owned domains and branding controls without thereby owning all
  domain-family truth
- platform control-plane uses domain management and cross-tenant oversight without thereby owning
  the whole family
- onboarding/provisioning establishes entry and first-owner handoff conditions that depend on route
  and tenant resolution without thereby defining the enduring cross-cutting family
- B2B, B2C, Aggregator-adjacent, and messaging surfaces all consume routed or branded entry
  contexts without thereby defining the whole family
- generic custom-domain presence, shell routing, branded storefront presence, or discoverability
  language is real evidence, but not by itself canonical family authority

Those are all real evidence surfaces. None of them alone is the canonical whole-family definition.

## 3. Lawful Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management Classification

The controlling classification is:

- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management is a distinct cross-cutting enabling family
- this family is not a governed commercial access model
- this family is not identical to white-label overlay, identity/workspace continuity, tenant back
  office, platform control-plane, onboarding/provisioning, B2B exchange, B2C storefront
  continuity, Aggregator handoff, or messaging/notifications
- this family defines the lawful continuity by which the correct tenant, brand surface, branded
  route context, and domain-bound presentation path are resolved and preserved across supported
  operating contexts without replacing the family reached through that route
- this family is bounded by current repo posture and therefore must not be rewritten as a router
  redesign, DNS/infra execution plan, auth rewrite, or runtime completion claim

Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management therefore exists as the cross-cutting family
that carries lawful tenant-resolution, branded entry, route-context continuity, and brand-surface
preservation across supported platform surfaces without replacing the parent or adjacent family that
consumes that routed or branded context.

## 4. What Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management Means In TexQtic Planning Truth

Within TexQtic planning truth, `Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management` means the
shared cross-cutting family of entry-path and surface-resolution continuity by which the correct
tenant, correct branded surface, and correct route-bound presentation context are reached and kept
coherent across lawful operating contexts.

At the family level, this means:

- tenant/domain-bound route resolution that determines which tenant or branded surface a user is
  entering
- brand-surface continuity that preserves the correct branded runtime or presentation path once
  that surface is resolved
- custom-domain and branded entry-path continuity where repo truth supports them
- route-context continuity needed so adjacent families can be reached through the correct branded
  or tenant-resolved surface
- the distinction between owning the route/brand-surface seam and owning the business family that
  appears on the other side of that seam

The correct high-level family statement is:

- Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management is the cross-cutting enabling family that
  preserves truthful tenant resolution, branded route context, and brand-surface continuity across
  lawful operating contexts without replacing overlay, identity, tenant-admin, control-plane,
  onboarding, parent commercial access model, or messaging-family ownership.

## 5. What Belongs Inside Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management Scope

The following belong inside the domain/routing/brand-surface family boundary:

### 5.1 Tenant/domain-bound route resolution

- public or authenticated tenant resolution where the purpose is to reach the correct tenant or
  branded operating container
- slug-, email-, domain-, or equivalent route-entry continuity where repo truth supports tenant
  lookup or tenant selection behavior
- route-resolution continuity that keeps entry targeted at the correct tenant or surface rather
  than a generic shell alone

### 5.2 Brand-surface continuity

- preservation of the correct branded runtime or presentation surface once the tenant/path context
  is resolved
- continuity between a lawful brand surface and the runtime or admin surface attached to it
- branded storefront, branded operator, or other brand-surface continuity where the family concern
  is the surface boundary rather than the whole adjacent family reached through it

### 5.3 Custom-domain and branded entry-path continuity

- custom-domain continuity where repo truth supports verification, management, or use of branded
  entry paths
- branded entry-path continuity tied to reaching the correct tenant-owned or overlay-owned surface
- domain-bound presentation-path continuity that preserves truthful brand targeting without
  implying broad infrastructure execution completeness

### 5.4 Route-context continuity across operating surfaces

- continuity that keeps the correct route context during entry, restore, handoff, or surface switch
- preservation of which runtime shell or branded surface should render after lawful resolution
- bounded continuity between public-safe entry, branded entry, and later authenticated surfaces
  where the route/surface seam itself is the concern

### 5.5 Cross-family dependency posture for routed and branded entry

- white-label dependence on branded delivery paths without turning WL into the owner of the whole
  family
- identity/workspace dependence on correct tenant resolution and surface targeting without turning
  identity into the owner of the whole family
- tenant-admin and control-plane dependence on domain and brand-surface visibility without turning
  either admin family into the owner of the whole family
- onboarding/provisioning dependence on lawful entry targets and prepared surface continuity
  without turning onboarding into the owner of the whole family

## 6. What Does Not Belong Inside Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management Scope

The following are adjacent or later families and must not be silently absorbed into domain-family
design:

### 6.1 White-Label Overlay as a whole family

- branded storefront/runtime behavior, WL admin posture, or overlay deployment behavior as a whole-
  family substitute
- any attempt to define all route and brand-surface truth through white-label ownership alone

### 6.2 Identity / Tenancy / Permissions / Workspace Continuity as a whole family

- realm, session, membership, permission, or workspace continuity as a whole-family substitute
- tenant identity or workspace entry treated as if it defines all route and brand-surface truth in
  full

### 6.3 Tenant Back Office as a whole family

- tenant-owned branding, settings, integrations, or admin controls as a whole-family substitute
- tenant-owned domain/configuration controls treated as if they define the entire cross-cutting
  family

### 6.4 Platform Control-Plane as a whole family

- cross-tenant supervision, tenant registry, domain oversight, or governance operations as a whole-
  family substitute
- control-plane domain-management posture treated as if it defines the entire family

### 6.5 Onboarding / Tenant Provisioning / CRM Coordination as a whole family

- provisioning workflow, business verification, first-owner handoff, or CRM coordination as a
  whole-family substitute
- prepared entry targets or onboarding transitions treated as if they define the whole enduring
  route/brand family

### 6.6 B2B Exchange Core, B2C, or Aggregator as whole families

- authenticated business exchange posture, tenant-branded consumer-commerce posture, or
  directory/handoff posture as whole-family substitutes
- parent commercial access model entry or presentation treated as if it defines the full route /
  brand-surface family

### 6.7 Messaging / Notifications as a whole family

- message delivery context, alert surfaces, or communication routing treated as if they define the
  whole domain/routing family
- notification reachability treated as a substitute for route/brand-surface ownership

### 6.8 Generic presence or implementation work

- custom-domain presence, branded shell presence, router state, storefront configuration panels, or
  discoverability copy treated as whole-family proof
- DNS/infra execution, host-header execution, auth rewrite, router rewrite, schema redesign, API
  redesign, or runtime completion claims

## 7. Relationship To Adjacent Families

### 7.1 Relationship to White-Label Overlay

- White-label depends heavily on branded delivery paths, routed entry, and brand-surface
  continuity.
- White-label does not define the whole domain/routing/brand-surface family.
- Overlay-specific branded surfaces are evidence of adjacent reuse, not canonical ownership of the
  full cross-cutting family.

### 7.2 Relationship to Identity / Tenancy / Permissions / Workspace Continuity

- Identity/workspace continuity depends on lawful tenant resolution and correct route-bound entry
  into the right operating context.
- Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management does not define realm, membership,
  permissions, or workspace continuity in full.
- Tenant resolution and route context must remain distinct from the broader identity family.

### 7.3 Relationship to Tenant Back Office

- Tenant back office may expose tenant-owned branding, domain configuration, and related admin
  controls in bounded form.
- Tenant back office does not define the whole domain/routing/brand-surface family.
- Tenant-owned controls are evidence of adjacent reuse, not canonical ownership of all route and
  brand-surface continuity.

### 7.4 Relationship to Platform Control-Plane

- Platform control-plane may supervise tenant domains, verification, and cross-tenant domain
  management posture.
- Platform control-plane does not define the whole domain/routing/brand-surface family.
- Control-plane domain oversight is evidence of adjacent reuse, not canonical ownership of the full
  cross-cutting family.

### 7.5 Relationship to Onboarding / Tenant Provisioning / CRM Coordination

- Onboarding and provisioning may instantiate the initial entry target, tenant-bound route context,
  or first-owner handoff path that later uses this family.
- The domain/routing/brand-surface family begins where that prepared state must remain coherent as
  an entry and surface-resolution seam.
- Onboarding/provisioning does not define the whole enduring cross-cutting family.

### 7.6 Relationship to B2B Exchange Core

- B2B may consume tenant resolution and route context to reach the correct authenticated business
  exchange surface.
- Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management does not define the whole B2B family.
- Exchange entry surfaces are adjacent evidence, not whole-family ownership.

### 7.7 Relationship to B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce

- B2C depends on branded storefront entry, routed brand surfaces, and public-safe tenant-targeted
  entry continuity.
- Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management does not define the whole B2C family.
- B2C storefront entry is adjacent evidence, not canonical ownership of the full cross-cutting
  family.

### 7.8 Relationship to Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff

- Aggregator may consume tenant resolution or routed entry to reach the correct discovery and
  handoff surface.
- Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management does not define the whole Aggregator family.
- Aggregator-adjacent resolution surfaces are adjacent evidence, not canonical ownership.

### 7.9 Relationship to Messaging / Notifications

- Messaging may depend on route and brand-surface context for lawful delivery, reachability, or
  branded presentation.
- Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management does not define the whole messaging family.
- Communication reachability must remain distinct from route and brand-surface ownership.

## 8. Current Sources Of Drift This Family Design Is Meant To Stop

This family design is specifically meant to stop the following recurring planning errors:

- treating white-label overlay as if it owns all domain, routing, and brand-surface truth
- treating identity/workspace entry and tenant resolution as if they define the whole family
- treating tenant-owned domains/branding controls as if tenant back office owns the whole family
- treating control-plane domain-management or tenant-details surfaces as if platform supervision
  owns the whole family
- treating onboarding/provisioning entry handoff as if it defines the enduring cross-cutting
  family
- treating generic custom-domain presence, branded-shell presence, or route behavior as sufficient
  proof of lawful family definition
- widening domain-family planning into DNS, infra, router, auth, or runtime execution redesign

## 9. Inheritance Rules For Later Planning

Later units should inherit the following rules from this family anchor:

- if a later family needs tenant resolution, branded route context, or brand-surface continuity, it
  should cite this family rather than silently redefining it
- no later family may claim domain/routing-family ownership merely because it exposes custom-domain
  controls, branded surfaces, or route-context-sensitive behavior
- white-label may heavily depend on this family without replacing it as the cross-cutting authority
- identity/workspace may enable lawful tenant resolution and entry without replacing this family as
  the route/brand authority
- tenant back office and control-plane may expose bounded domain-management surfaces without
  replacing this family as the cross-cutting authority
- later runtime or implementation work must stay bounded to its local problem and must not claim to
  have redesigned the whole route/brand-surface family unless explicitly opened as such

## 10. What Remains Deferred After This Anchor

This anchor resolves the family-classification gap only.

It does not by itself resolve:

- DNS or custom-domain execution work
- router/runtime design work
- auth or realm rewrite work
- schema or API redesign for domain management
- branded-surface implementation completion
- any production proof that every routed or branded surface is materially complete

Those remain separate follow-on concerns if and when later units explicitly open them.
