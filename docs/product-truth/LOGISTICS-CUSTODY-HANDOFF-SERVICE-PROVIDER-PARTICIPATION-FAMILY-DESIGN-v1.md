## 3. Lawful Logistics / Custody Handoff / Service-Provider Participation Classification

The controlling classification is:

- logistics / custody handoff / service-provider participation is a distinct top-level supply-chain
  execution family
- this family is not a governed commercial access model
- this family is not identical to orders / checkout / post-purchase continuity
- this family is not identical to the deferred fulfillment / shipment / returns family
- this family is not identical to compliance / DPP, platform control-plane, or Aggregator
  discovery/handoff ownership

This family therefore exists as the top-level continuity by which physical custody transfer,
handoff accountability, and logistics-service-provider participation remain inspectable and
attributable across lawful TexQtic trade execution without being collapsed into adjacent families.

## 4. What Logistics / Custody Handoff / Service-Provider Participation Means In TexQtic Planning Truth

Within TexQtic planning truth, `Logistics / Custody Handoff / Service-Provider Participation`
means the shared supply-chain execution family of custody-boundary continuity and logistics-actor
participation by which physical movement and handoff of governed goods remain reviewable across
supplier, carrier, intermediary, and buyer-adjacent contexts.

At the family level, this means:

- custody-boundary continuity across shipment, receipt, handoff, and ownership-transfer seams
  where current doctrine supports them
- logistics partner or service-provider participation where external actors carry, receive, or
  transfer governed goods
- continuity between trade or batch evidence and custody-coverage semantics used by TCs and DPP
  regulatory-risk flags
- platform-visible but not platform-owned execution truth concerning who physically holds or
  transfers goods at each lawful boundary

The correct high-level family statement is:

- Logistics / Custody Handoff / Service-Provider Participation is the top-level supply-chain
  execution family that preserves truthful custody-boundary and logistics-actor continuity across
  lawful TexQtic trade execution without replacing orders, fulfillment, compliance/DPP, control-
  plane supervision, or Aggregator discovery ownership.

## 5. What Belongs Inside Logistics / Custody Handoff / Service-Provider Participation Scope

### 5.1 Custody-boundary continuity

- shipment, receipt, and handoff boundary determination where the concern is who has lawful
  custody of goods
- deterministic custody-boundary projection or read truth tied to trade progression
- batch-, trade-, or event-linked evidence sufficient to evaluate custody continuity

### 5.2 Logistics service-provider participation

- carrier, port, inspector, or equivalent service-provider participation where goods are moved,
  held, transferred, or formally handed off
- allowlisting or participation rules for logistics actors where current doctrine or later bounded
  units require them
- handoff-event continuity distinct from Aggregator discovery or profile visibility

### 5.3 Compliance and DPP adjacency

- TC-coverage semantics over custody boundaries
- DPP regulatory-risk visibility when custody coverage is missing or invalid
- audit-ready custody evidence reused by compliance-family and DPP subfamily surfaces without
  changing ownership of those adjacent families

### 5.4 Cross-family dependency posture

- order or trade lifecycle can create or depend on custody boundaries without absorbing this family
- later fulfillment-family work may consume logistics truth at the merchant/operator boundary
  without replacing this family
- platform control-plane may supervise logistics-adjacent risk or casework without becoming the
  owner of logistics-family truth
- Aggregator may later route or hand off into service-provider actors without becoming the owner of
  custody-boundary continuity

## 6. What Does Not Belong Inside Logistics / Custody Handoff / Service-Provider Participation Scope

### 6.1 Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase as a whole family

- cart, checkout, order creation, or bounded post-purchase continuity as a whole-family substitute
- downstream order-state truth treated as if it defines physical custody continuity in full

### 6.2 Fulfillment / Shipment / Returns as a merchant-side execution family

- merchant/operator dispatch, after-order execution, or returns-loop ownership as a whole-family
  substitute
- seller-side post-order orchestration treated as if it defines external logistics participation in
  full

### 6.3 Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit or DPP as whole-family substitutes

- regulator-facing disclosure, audit-read layers, or certification evidence as the whole-family
  substitute for logistics continuity
- DPP risk flags treated as if they define the custody family rather than reuse it

### 6.4 Platform Control-Plane as a whole family

- cross-tenant supervision, disputes, finance oversight, or casework as the whole-family substitute
- platform governance visibility treated as if it owns physical logistics continuity itself

### 6.5 Payments / Escrow / Settlement or commercial access modes as whole-family substitutes

- finance-state progression, escrow governance, settlement-state ownership, or fee visibility as a
  substitute for custody continuity
- B2B, B2C, or Aggregator commercial entry posture treated as if it defines logistics-family truth

### 6.6 Implementation, marketplace, or partner-program redesign

- implementation sequencing, schema changes, API work, or runtime completion claims
- broad carrier marketplace, partner-program, or vendor-network redesign beyond the minimum family
  boundary defined here
