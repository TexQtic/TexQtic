The required distinction now is not scope inclusion. The required distinction is planning posture:

- `LOCKED IN FOR LAUNCH` but `DESIGN-GATE REQUIRED FIRST`

next for each family. Without that split, TexQtic would risk treating:

- mixed commercial truth as normalized
- routed B2C presence as a finished launch loop
- bounded platform operations as a complete admin redesign
- routed Aggregator presence as implementation-ready

## 3. Planning-Split Rule

Required launch families are split by planning posture, not by feature importance.

The governing rule is:

- if a family is already bounded, truthful, and specific enough to support implementation-design
  planning without first reconciling mixed truth or defining a missing operating model, it goes to
  `LANE A — IMPLEMENTATION-DESIGN READY AFTER SCOPE LOCK`
- if a family is retained in launch scope but current repo truth is mixed, inconsistent, decorative,
  fake-complete, or commercially incoherent, it goes to
  `LANE B — NORMALIZATION ARTIFACT REQUIRED FIRST`
- if a family is retained in launch scope but the bounded target operating model is still
  under-defined, it goes to `LANE C — DESIGN-GATE ARTIFACT REQUIRED FIRST`

These lanes are not deferral lanes.

All three lanes remain inside the launch program.
The lane only states what planning artifact must come next before implementation-design planning can
truthfully begin.

## 6. Ordering Rationale

The queue is ordered by planning posture dependency, not by user-visible surface importance.

### Why Aggregator comes first

Aggregator comes first because it is the only locked launch family whose exact bounded operating
model is still under-defined at the family level.

That makes it a `DESIGN-GATE REQUIRED FIRST` problem rather than a normalization problem.
Until that design gate exists, any later artifact would risk describing the wrong launch promise,
the wrong workflow target, or the wrong implementation-design boundary.

### Why subscription/commercial truth comes before launch language relies on it

Subscription/commercial packaging comes next because repo truth already shows mixed commercial
vocabulary and partial enforcement:

- DB plan values exist
- frontend plan normalization differs
- AI budget controls are real
- broad entitlement gating is not materially evidenced

This means commercial packaging is already important to launch truth, but not yet coherent enough
to support implementation-design planning or external launch language without a normalization pass.

### Why B2C continuity is a real pre-launch obligation

B2C continuity comes next because B2C is now explicitly retained in launch scope.

That changes its status from a later-ready nice-to-have into a real pre-launch obligation.
The problem is still bounded and continuity-focused rather than strategy-wide, so its next artifact
should be a normalization note rather than a broad redesign stream.

### Why platform-ops truth is bounded rather than a redesign stream

Platform-ops launch boundary comes after the prior three because current repo truth already shows a
materially real operator/control-center stack. The problem is not absence of platform operations.
The problem is boundary truth:

- what can be promised now
- what is still thin or fake-complete
- where admin-authority claims remain security-sensitive or separately gated

That makes the next artifact a boundary-normalization artifact, not a full redesign roadmap.

### Can any artifact proceed in parallel?

Yes, but conservatively.

- Aggregator design-gate work can proceed in parallel with subscription normalization because the
  two artifacts solve different truth classes: undefined operating model versus mixed commercial
  vocabulary.
- B2C continuity and platform-ops boundary artifacts can also proceed in parallel with one another
  once the queue start is clear, because they are separate bounded continuity/boundary problems.

The queue remains ordered because parallelizable does not mean equally foundational.

## 7. Parallelism vs Strict Sequence

Strictly earlier:

- `Aggregator operating model design-gate artifact` must come before any Aggregator
  implementation-design planning.
- `Subscription / entitlement normalization artifact` must come before launch language or later
  implementation-design planning relies on commercial tiers or entitlements.

Can be planned in parallel:

- `Subscription / entitlement normalization artifact`
- `B2C launch continuity artifact`
- `Platform-ops launch boundary artifact`

These three can proceed in parallel because they address separate bounded truth problems and do not
share a required target-state definition.

Should wait until earlier work completes:

- Any Aggregator implementation-design planning should wait until the Aggregator design gate exists.
- Any launch claim that depends on commercial packaging should wait until subscription normalization
  is complete.
- Any B2C implementation-design planning that assumes a finished launch promise should wait until
  B2C continuity is normalized.
- Any platform-admin implementation-design planning that assumes broad tenant deep-dive or broader
  admin-authority completeness should wait until the platform-ops boundary artifact is complete.
