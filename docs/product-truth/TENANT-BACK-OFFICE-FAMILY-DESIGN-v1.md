## 1. Purpose and Authority

This artifact is the canonical current family-level design anchor for `Tenant Back Office`.

Its purpose is narrow:

- define the lawful tenant back-office family under the adopted canonical model
- separate tenant-owned administrative and operational continuity from parent commercial access
  models, white-label overlay, and platform control-plane supervision
- give later family-specific units one reusable tenant-admin boundary so future planning does not
  keep reconstructing it from shell presence or mixed mode language

This artifact does not authorize implementation, does not redesign adjacent families, and does not
replace Layer 0 governance truth.

## 2. Current Controlling Posture

Current repo authority already fixes the following truths:

- TexQtic is the operating system for trusted textile supply chains.
- The only governed commercial access models are B2B Exchange, B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce, and
  Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff.
- White-label is an overlay capability and deployment/experience model, not a separate commercial
  access model.
- Enterprise is subordinate depth within B2B, not a separate platform mode.
- Control-plane, superadmin, and WL-admin surfaces are governance/operations surfaces, not
  commercial pillars.
- Tenant-owned memberships, branding, and basic operator controls are materially evidenced now,
  but their family-level planning authority remains diffuse.

The remaining planning gap is not whether tenant-owned admin continuity exists. The gap is that it
is still described indirectly through mode-specific matrices, WL operator notes, launch-readiness
phrasing, or shell presence instead of through one explicit tenant back-office family anchor.

## 3. Lawful Tenant Back Office Classification

The controlling classification is:

- tenant back office is a family of tenant-owned administrative and operational continuity
- tenant back office is not a governed commercial access model
- tenant back office is not a control-plane or platform supervision family
- tenant back office is not a substitute for white-label overlay or for any parent commercial access
  model

Tenant back office therefore exists as a cross-mode tenant-owned operating family that can support
B2B, B2C, Aggregator, and overlay-enabled tenant contexts without changing their parent
classification.

## 4. What Tenant Back Office Means In TexQtic Planning Truth

Within TexQtic planning truth, `Tenant Back Office` means the tenant-owned administrative and
operational surfaces required for an organization to run its workspace coherently inside TexQtic.

At the family level, this means:

- organization profile and workspace administration owned by the tenant
- team, member, role, and staff administration at tenant scope
- tenant-owned settings, configuration, and operational controls that do not belong to platform
  supervision
- tenant-owned integrations and workspace-operating configuration where those concerns are not part
  of the separate platform control-plane or identity families
- tenant-facing brand/store/admin continuity where it is tenant-owned but not sufficient to define
  the whole white-label family
- shared or analogous administrative continuity across B2B, B2C, Aggregator, and overlay-enabled
  tenant contexts

The correct high-level family statement is:

- Tenant Back Office is the tenant-owned administrative and operational family that lets an
  organization manage its own workspace coherently across lawful parent contexts without replacing
  parent mode or overlay classification.

## 5. What Belongs Inside Tenant Back Office Scope

The following belong inside the tenant back-office family boundary:

### 5.1 Organization and workspace administration

- org profile and tenant-owned workspace identity administration
- tenant-owned basic workspace settings and configuration
- workspace-operating continuity needed for a tenant to manage itself as an organization

### 5.2 Team, members, and tenant-owned staff administration

- membership and staff administration at tenant scope
- tenant-owned role and access administration in bounded tenant-facing form
- invite, manage, and maintain tenant staff continuity where it belongs to the tenant workspace

### 5.3 Tenant-owned branding, settings, and configuration

- branding and store/profile configuration where the tenant owns the surface
- tenant-owned settings and operator controls needed to keep the workspace truthful and usable
- bounded domain or configuration concerns where they are part of tenant-owned workspace operation
  rather than full domain-family ownership

### 5.4 Tenant-owned integrations and operational configuration

- tenant-facing integrations configuration
- workspace-operating hooks and settings that support tenant workflow rather than platform-wide
  governance
- bounded operational configuration needed for a tenant to run its own environment coherently

### 5.5 Cross-mode tenant administrative continuity

- tenant-facing admin continuity that appears across B2B, B2C, Aggregator, and overlay-enabled
  contexts
- analogous tenant-owned back-office responsibilities even where depth differs by parent context
- tenant-owned administrative surfaces that should not be inferred from shell presence alone

## 6. What Does Not Belong Inside Tenant Back Office Scope

The following are adjacent or later families and must not be silently absorbed into tenant
back-office design:

### 6.1 B2B Exchange Core

- the full B2B exchange family
- RFQ, negotiation, trade, order, escrow, settlement, compliance, and downstream exchange workflow
  as a whole-family substitute for tenant back office
- enterprise depth classification

### 6.2 B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce

- the full B2C family definition
- public-safe browse-entry, consumer checkout, post-purchase, or storefront-family truth as a
  substitute for tenant back-office scope

### 6.3 Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff

- the full Aggregator family definition
- directory/discovery, network routing, or handoff ownership as a substitute for tenant-admin scope

### 6.4 White-Label Overlay as a whole family

- white-label branded storefront/runtime behavior as a whole-family definition
- overlay-specific operator surfaces taken as proof of the whole tenant back-office family
- WL-admin treated as if it defines all tenant-admin continuity across modes

### 6.5 Platform control-plane / superadmin / platform admin

- tenant registry, cross-tenant supervision, onboarding review, activation oversight, audit
  visibility, finance oversight, disputes, compliance casework, or global administration
- tenant deep-dive supervision treated as a tenant-owned back-office concern
- platform-level RBAC, escalation, or governance operations

### 6.6 Identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace continuity as a whole family

- the full cross-cutting identity and workspace family
- platform-wide tenancy rules, permissions architecture, or workspace-continuity ownership in full
- any attempt to solve the entire identity family through tenant back-office definition alone

### 6.7 Domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management as a whole family

- full domain-family ownership
- tenant resolution, routing, and brand-surface management as a whole cross-cutting family
- any attempt to fully solve domain/routing through tenant back-office design alone

### 6.8 Catalog, orders, payments, messaging, or implementation work as whole-family substitutes

- catalog/discovery cross-mode family ownership
- orders / checkout / post-purchase family ownership
- payments / escrow / settlement family ownership
- messaging / notifications family ownership
- implementation planning or runtime completion work

## 7. Relationship To Adjacent Families

### 7.1 Relationship to B2B Exchange Core

- Tenant back office can support B2B tenant operation, but it does not replace B2B as the parent
  commercial access model.
- Tenant-owned admin continuity must not be read as proof that the whole B2B exchange family is
  defined or complete.

### 7.2 Relationship to B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce

- Tenant back office can support B2C operator administration in bounded form.
- Tenant back office does not define the whole B2C family or broader storefront truth.

### 7.3 Relationship to Aggregator

- Tenant back office can support Aggregator tenant administration where lawful.
- Aggregator discovery, network, and handoff truth remain separate from tenant-admin ownership.

### 7.4 Relationship to White-Label Overlay

- White-label can include overlay-owned operator or admin surfaces.
- Those overlay-owned surfaces may overlap with tenant back-office only where they are tenant-owned
  administrative continuity.
- White-label overlay remains a separate family and WL-admin does not define the whole tenant
  back-office family.

### 7.5 Relationship to WL-admin

- WL-admin is a specific operator/admin surface related to the white-label overlay.
- WL-admin may contribute evidence of tenant-owned administrative continuity in overlay contexts.
- WL-admin is not the canonical definition of tenant back office across the platform.

### 7.6 Relationship to platform control-plane / superadmin

- Platform control-plane remains the platform supervision and oversight family.
- Tenant back office remains tenant-owned and tenant-scoped rather than cross-tenant and platform-
  supervisory.
- Tenant deep-dive, platform audit, finance, disputes, and global admin concerns remain outside
  this family.

### 7.7 Relationship to identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace continuity

- identity, tenancy, permissions, and workspace continuity overlap with tenant back office because
  tenant staff and workspace settings depend on them
- the full cross-cutting identity/workspace family remains separate and must not be collapsed into
  this family anchor

### 7.8 Relationship to domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management

- domain and routing concerns may overlap with tenant-owned configuration and brand surfaces
- the full domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management family remains separate and later

## 8. Current Sources Of Drift

The main current drift classes are:

- the tenant dashboard matrix acting as descriptive evidence of admin domains without separately
  fixing the family boundary
- launch-readiness phrasing that groups tenant admin, WL admin, and control-plane admin surfaces in
  one mixed posture statement
- WL-specific back-office language that can make overlay-owned admin look like the whole tenant
  admin story
- shell or panel presence being treated as if it defines a lawful family boundary
- older implementation-planning language that juxtaposes tenant admin dashboard completion and WL
  admin completion without a stable cross-mode tenant back-office definition

These sources can remain useful as evidence of runtime truth, but they must not serve as the
canonical family-classification authority going forward.

## 9. Inheritance Rules For Later Family Units

Later family-specific units must inherit all of the following:

1. Tenant back office is a tenant-owned administrative and operational family, not a commercial
   access model.
2. Tenant back office can support B2B, B2C, Aggregator, and overlay-enabled contexts without
   replacing the parent model or overlay classification.
3. WL-admin or overlay-owned operator surfaces must not be used to define the whole tenant
   back-office family.
4. Platform control-plane work must remain separate from tenant-owned back-office continuity.
5. Identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace work must remain a distinct later cross-cutting
   family unless a later unit explicitly narrows itself to a tenant-facing subset.
6. Domain / tenant routing / brand-surface work must remain a distinct later family even where it
   overlaps with tenant-owned configuration.
7. Tenant back-office work must not silently absorb B2B, B2C, Aggregator, catalog, orders,
   payments, messaging, or implementation-family truth.
8. Family boundary truth must not be inferred from shell presence or partial panel depth alone.

## 10. Deferred To Later Units

This family anchor deliberately does not solve:

- platform control-plane replanning
- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace family design
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management family design
- B2C broader family design
- catalog / discovery cross-mode design
- orders / checkout / post-purchase family design
- payments / escrow / settlement family design
- messaging / notifications family design
- implementation planning or runtime changes

Those remain later bounded units.

## 11. Readiness Outcome

Result of this artifact:

- tenant back office now has a current explicit family-level planning/design anchor
- future planning can no longer lawfully collapse tenant admin/back-office into WL-admin,
  platform control-plane, or parent mode truth without violating current repo authority
- adjacent-family boundaries are explicit enough that later units can inherit them without
  widening into control-plane, identity, domain, B2B, B2C, Aggregator, or implementation work
