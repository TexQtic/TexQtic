## 1. Purpose and Authority

This artifact is the canonical current family-level design anchor for
`Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity`.

Its purpose is narrow:

- define the lawful cross-mode catalog/discovery/product-data family under the adopted canonical
  model
- separate shared product-data continuity from parent commercial access model ownership,
  white-label presentation, tenant back-office administration, and platform control-plane
  governance
- give later family-specific units one reusable cross-mode boundary so future planning does not
  keep inferring catalog-family truth from mode-local browse surfaces, tenant catalog CRUD work,
  or Aggregator directory language alone

This artifact does not authorize implementation, does not redesign adjacent families, and does not
replace Layer 0 governance truth.

## 2. Current Controlling Posture

Current repo authority already fixes the following truths:

- TexQtic is the operating system for trusted textile supply chains.
- The only governed commercial access models are B2B Exchange, B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce, and
  Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff.
- White-label is an overlay capability and deployment/experience model, not a separate commercial
  access model.
- Enterprise is subordinate depth within B2B, not a separate platform mode.
- Control-plane, superadmin, and WL-admin surfaces are governance/operations surfaces, not
  commercial pillars.
- Catalog-backed browse, tenant catalog ownership, and mode-specific discovery surfaces are all
  materially evidenced now, but their family-level planning authority remains fragmented.

The remaining planning gap is not whether catalog or discovery continuity exists. The gap is that
current repo truth still describes it through neighboring families:

- B2B uses catalog continuity as part of governed exchange posture.
- B2C uses catalog-backed browse preview as part of storefront-entry truth.
- Aggregator uses directory/discovery language for qualified counterparty finding and intent
  handoff.
- white-label uses products, collections, and presentation as overlay-owned runtime.
- tenant catalog CRUD artifacts describe one bounded tenant-owned lifecycle seam.

Those are all real evidence surfaces. None of them alone is the canonical whole-family definition.

## 3. Lawful Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Classification

The controlling classification is:

- catalog / discovery / product data continuity is a cross-mode family of product-information,
  listing, browse, search, and discoverability continuity
- this family is not a governed commercial access model
- this family is not identical to B2B exchange, B2C storefront continuity, or Aggregator
  directory-and-handoff ownership
- this family is not identical to tenant back-office administration, white-label overlay, or
  platform control-plane supervision

Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity therefore exists as the cross-mode product-data and
discoverability family that supplies truthful product/counterparty information continuity to lawful
parent contexts without replacing their parent classification.

## 4. What Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity Means In TexQtic Planning Truth

Within TexQtic planning truth, `Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity` means the shared
family of product-information and discoverability continuity that lets tenants and users encounter,
inspect, and act on truthful catalog or directory-facing data across lawful parent contexts.

At the family level, this means:

- product and catalog data that can be created, maintained, read, and presented truthfully
- discovery and browse continuity that exposes items, counterparties, or capabilities through a
  materially real data-backed surface rather than decorative copy alone
- shared search, filter, list, detail, collection, or preview continuity where those behaviors are
  primarily about finding and inspecting truthful data
- product-data continuity between tenant-owned catalog management and downstream read surfaces that
  consume that data
- bounded counterparty or capability discovery continuity where discovery is data-backed but does
  not itself own downstream transaction execution

The correct high-level family statement is:

- Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity is the cross-mode family that preserves truthful
  product, catalog, and discoverability continuity across lawful parent contexts without replacing
  exchange ownership, storefront ownership, overlay ownership, or directory-handoff ownership.

## 5. What Belongs Inside Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity Scope

The following belong inside the catalog/discovery/product-data family boundary:

### 5.1 Product-data and catalog truth

- tenant-owned product and catalog records as they relate to truthful downstream read continuity
- product attributes, commercial display fields, capability summaries, and related read-facing data
  required to make discovery or inspection truthful
- bounded collection, grouping, and preview continuity where the purpose is to expose truthful
  discoverability rather than to define a whole parent mode

### 5.2 Browse, search, list, and detail continuity

- catalog-backed browse and list continuity
- search, filter, and inspection continuity where the main purpose is to find or inspect truthful
  product/capability data
- item-level or counterparty-level detail continuity where the primary concern is truthful data
  presentation and inspection

### 5.3 Cross-mode product-data reuse and continuity

- shared product-data continuity reused across B2B, B2C, white-label-enabled surfaces, and bounded
  Aggregator discovery contexts
- continuity between tenant-owned catalog management and public-safe or authenticated read-facing
  discovery surfaces
- product-data transport or read support that makes discoverability materially real without
  redefining the parent family that consumes it

### 5.4 Discovery truthfulness as a family concern

- the distinction between workflow-backed discovery and decorative discovery copy
- ensuring that browse/discovery surfaces are backed by truthful data rather than static placeholder
  language alone
- bounded discoverability rules that prevent open-bazaar or undifferentiated-listing drift

## 6. What Does Not Belong Inside Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity Scope

The following are adjacent or later families and must not be silently absorbed into catalog-family
design:

### 6.1 B2B Exchange Core as a whole family

- governed commercial engagement, RFQ, negotiation, trade, order, escrow, settlement, and
  downstream exchange workflow as the whole B2B family
- B2B-specific authenticated exchange posture taken as if it were the whole catalog family
- enterprise depth classification

### 6.2 B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce as a whole family

- the whole B2C browse-entry, cart, checkout, order, and post-purchase family definition
- public storefront continuity taken as if it defines the whole catalog/discovery family
- consumer-commerce strategy or full merchandising posture as a substitute for cross-mode catalog
  truth

### 6.3 Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff as a whole family

- Aggregator-owned qualification, inquiry capture, and intent-routing ownership as a whole-family
  substitute
- the bounded handoff from directory discovery into downstream exchange execution
- any attempt to redefine the entire catalog family as Aggregator directory behavior

### 6.4 White-Label Overlay as a whole family

- branded storefront/runtime presentation behavior as a whole-family substitute
- white-label operator/admin product presentation ownership treated as if it defines all catalog
  truth
- overlay branding or domain presentation as a substitute for shared product-data continuity

### 6.5 Tenant Back Office as a whole family

- tenant-owned org administration, memberships, settings, branding administration, and integrations
- tenant-admin ownership of catalog controls taken as if it defines the entire catalog family
- generic tenant back-office shell presence treated as catalog-family proof

### 6.6 Platform Control-Plane as a whole family

- cross-tenant supervision, audit, compliance casework, finance oversight, feature governance, and
  operator controls
- platform visibility into catalogs or tenants treated as if it defines the catalog family

### 6.7 Orders, payments, messaging, identity, domain, or implementation families

- orders / checkout / post-purchase family ownership
- payments / escrow / settlement family ownership
- messaging / notifications family ownership
- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace family ownership
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management family ownership
- implementation planning, runtime changes, schema work, media/upload redesign, or search-engine
  rearchitecture

## 7. Relationship To Adjacent Families

### 7.1 Relationship to B2B Exchange Core

- B2B uses catalog and governed discovery continuity as part of exchange.
- The catalog family supplies truthful product/discovery continuity without replacing B2B exchange
  ownership.
- Cross-mode catalog work must preserve B2B's authenticated business-exchange posture rather than
  flattening it into public directory semantics.

### 7.2 Relationship to B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce

- B2C uses catalog-backed browse preview and later storefront continuity.
- The catalog family may support B2C browse and product inspection, but it does not define the
  whole B2C family.
- B2C storefront CTA continuity, checkout, and post-purchase remain outside this family.

### 7.3 Relationship to Aggregator

- Aggregator uses bounded directory/counterparty discovery and intent handoff.
- The catalog family can support truthful discovery data for Aggregator contexts, but Aggregator
  still owns qualification and handoff semantics inside its own bounded family.
- The distinction between data-backed discovery and downstream handoff must remain explicit.

### 7.4 Relationship to White-Label Overlay

- white-label may present products, collections, and branded discovery differently.
- The catalog family supplies truthful underlying product-data continuity without turning branded
  presentation into the whole family definition.
- Overlay-owned admin/product presentation remains separate from the whole catalog family.

### 7.5 Relationship to Tenant Back Office

- tenant back office can own catalog administration controls in bounded tenant-owned form.
- Catalog-family truth concerns the continuity of product/discovery data itself, not the entire
  tenant-admin family.
- Tenant catalog CRUD work is evidence of one bounded seam, not the whole family definition.

### 7.6 Relationship to Platform Control-Plane

- platform control-plane may supervise or inspect tenant/catalog posture in bounded form.
- That does not make control-plane the owner of catalog/discovery/product-data family truth.

## 8. Current Sources Of Drift

The main current drift classes are:

- the B2B family anchor lawfully includes commercial and catalog continuity, which can make B2B
  exchange truth look like the whole catalog family if read too broadly
- the B2C launch continuity artifact treats catalog-backed browse preview as one bounded public
  storefront truth, which can make B2C browse-entry look like the whole family
- the Aggregator normalization and implementation-design artifacts center directory discovery and
  counterparty inspection, which can make discovery truth look Aggregator-owned by default
- the tenant dashboard matrix groups catalog under the shared `TS` domain and preserves an older
  `Catalog Hub` overhang for Aggregator, which can make mode-local dashboard modules look like the
  full family authority
- the older tenant catalog management continuity artifact describes one bounded tenant-owned
  lifecycle seam and can be misread as the canonical catalog-family anchor if not explicitly
  bounded
- white-label product, collection, and storefront surfaces can make branded presentation look like
  the core catalog family instead of one overlay expression

These sources remain useful evidence of runtime or bounded planning truth, but they must not serve
as the canonical family-classification authority going forward.

## 9. Inheritance Rules For Later Family Units

Later family-specific units must inherit all of the following:

1. Catalog / discovery / product data continuity is a cross-mode family, not a commercial access
   model.
2. Parent families may consume catalog/discovery continuity without replacing the family that
   supplies it.
3. Tenant catalog CRUD work must not be used to define the whole family.
4. B2C browse-entry work must remain separate unless a later unit explicitly narrows itself to a
   cross-mode product-data seam.
5. Aggregator discovery work must distinguish discovery data continuity from handoff ownership.
6. White-label product presentation must not be used as the definition of the whole family.
7. Catalog-family work must not silently absorb B2B exchange, B2C commerce, Aggregator handoff,
   tenant back-office, control-plane, orders, payments, messaging, identity, domain, or
   implementation-family truth.
8. Discovery surfaces must be judged against the anti-bazaar rule: they must support structured,
   permissioned, truthful discovery rather than open listing and undifferentiated directory drift.

## 10. Deferred To Later Units

This family anchor deliberately does not solve:

- B2C broader family design
- orders / checkout / post-purchase family design
- payments / escrow / settlement family design
- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace family design
- messaging / notifications family design
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management family design
- platform search architecture or ranking-model redesign
- image/media/upload family work
- implementation planning or runtime changes

Those remain later bounded units.

Result of this artifact:
