## 1. Purpose and Authority

This artifact is the canonical current family-level design anchor for
`Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit`.

Its purpose is narrow:

- define the lawful cross-cutting governance-evidence family under the adopted canonical model
- separate compliance, certifications, traceability, and audit continuity from platform control-
  plane ownership, B2B exchange ownership, downstream orders-family ownership, downstream
  payments-family ownership, and generic audit-log/compliance-queue/certification UI presence
- give later family-specific units one reusable evidence/governance boundary so future planning
  does not keep inferring whole-family truth from control-center modules, exchange trust language,
  traceability admin surfaces, or launch-readiness tables alone

This artifact does not authorize implementation, does not redesign adjacent families, and does not
replace Layer 0 governance truth.

## 2. Current Controlling Posture

Current repo authority already fixes the following truths:

- TexQtic is the operating system for trusted textile supply chains.
- The only governed commercial access models are B2B Exchange, B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce, and
  Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff.
- White-label is an overlay capability and deployment/experience model, not a separate commercial
  access model.
- Enterprise is subordinate depth within B2B, not a separate platform mode.
- Control-plane, superadmin, and WL-admin surfaces are governance/operations surfaces, not
  commercial pillars.
- Doctrine already requires that every trade interaction be audit-logged.
- Current repo truth already evidences compliance queue, disputes, audit logs, traceability admin,
  certification-anchored casework, and audit visibility as materially real governance surfaces.

The remaining planning gap is not whether governance-evidence continuity exists. The gap is that
current repo truth still describes it through neighboring families and evidence surfaces:

- platform control-plane supervises compliance, disputes, audit integrity, and platform-level
  review without thereby owning the whole cross-cutting family
- B2B exchange requires compliance-aware gating and auditable trade progression without thereby
  owning all certifications, traceability, and audit-family truth
- orders and payments can depend on evidence, status integrity, or downstream visibility without
  thereby becoming the owner of the governance-evidence family
- launch-readiness and current-state artifacts confirm material platform governance surfaces, but
  those inventories do not by themselves define the family boundary
- generic audit logs, certification panels, compliance queues, and traceability views are real
  evidence surfaces, but not by themselves canonical whole-family authority

Those are all real evidence surfaces. None of them alone is the canonical whole-family definition.

## 3. Lawful Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit Classification

The controlling classification is:

- compliance / certifications / traceability / audit is a distinct cross-cutting governance-
  evidence family
- this family is not a governed commercial access model
- this family is not identical to platform control-plane, B2B exchange, downstream orders-family
  continuity, downstream payments-family continuity, identity/workspace continuity, messaging, or
  routing/brand-surface continuity
- this family defines the lawful continuity by which trade-relevant governance state, evidence,
  certification posture, traceability posture, and audit integrity remain inspectable and coherent
  across supported operating contexts without replacing the family being governed or reviewed
- this family is bounded by current repo posture and therefore must not be rewritten as a policy-
  engine redesign, export redesign, schema redesign, or runtime completion claim

Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit therefore exists as the cross-cutting family
that carries lawful evidence, certification, traceability, and audit continuity across supported
platform surfaces without replacing the parent or downstream family whose business flow generated
that evidence.

## 4. What Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit Means In TexQtic Planning Truth

Within TexQtic planning truth, `Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit` means the
shared cross-cutting family of governance-evidence continuity by which lawful participants and
operators can preserve, inspect, review, and act against material certification state,
traceability state, and audit history attached to governed trade execution.

At the family level, this means:

- compliance continuity that preserves whether required governance checks, review posture, and
  intervention status remain materially attached to the trade or operating context they govern
- certification continuity that preserves whether required trust artifacts, verification state, or
  certification-backed casework remain coherent enough for adjacent families to behave lawfully
- traceability continuity that preserves provenance-, chain-, or execution-relevant evidence state
  without turning every consuming workflow into the owner of traceability-family truth
- audit continuity that preserves immutable or reviewable event history for trade and platform
  actions without redefining the business family that emitted those events
- evidence visibility reused across exchange, admin, and governance surfaces without turning any
  one consuming family into the owner of the whole governance-evidence family

The correct high-level family statement is:

- Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit is the cross-cutting governance-evidence
  family that preserves truthful certification posture, traceability continuity, and auditability
  across lawful operating contexts without replacing control-plane, exchange, order, finance,
  identity, messaging, or routing family ownership.

## 5. What Belongs Inside Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit Scope

The following belong inside the compliance/audit family boundary:

### 5.1 Compliance-state continuity

- governed compliance review, intervention, or task continuity where the main concern is whether
  required review state remains attached to the correct trade or operating context
- compliance casework continuity where repo truth already supports certification-anchored or
  supervision-backed review posture
- compliance-state visibility reused by adjacent families without collapsing ownership into those
  adjacent families

### 5.2 Certification continuity

- certification-backed trust posture where the family concern is the existence, status, review, or
  governance meaning of the certification artifact rather than the surrounding commercial workflow
- certification records, certification review continuity, and certification-linked casework where
  current repo truth supports them
- the continuity seam between certification state and the business family that depends on it

### 5.3 Traceability continuity

- traceability state and evidence continuity where the family concern is preserving inspectable
  provenance, chain, or lifecycle evidence
- traceability views or admin continuity insofar as they preserve governance-evidence truth rather
  than redefine fulfillment, order, or exchange ownership
- evidence continuity that lets adjacent families inspect lineage or proof state without becoming
  the whole traceability family

### 5.4 Audit integrity and reviewable event history

- audit-log continuity for trade-relevant and platform-relevant actions where the main concern is
  reviewability, integrity, and event-history truth
- immutable or reviewable audit visibility where repo truth supports administrative or trade audit
  inspection
- audit continuity attached to interventions such as impersonation, disputes, compliance tasks, or
  trade progression when the concern is evidence preservation rather than the whole operational
  family itself

### 5.5 Governance-evidence reuse across operating surfaces

- control-plane reuse of compliance, audit, traceability, and certification visibility without
  turning control-plane into the owner of the whole family
- B2B reuse of compliance-aware gating and auditable progression without turning B2B into the
  owner of the whole family
- downstream orders or finance reuse of evidence state where they depend on auditability or
  certification posture without becoming the family owner

### 5.6 DPP / regulator-facing disclosure as an explicit subfamily

`DPP / Regulator-Facing Disclosure` belongs inside this family as an explicit named subfamily.

At minimum, that subfamily means:

- regulator-facing read-layer continuity that assembles traceability, certification, audit, and
  evidence-backed risk posture into a lawful disclosure surface
- DPP snapshot/read views, disclosure packaging, and regulatory-risk visibility derived from this
  family rather than treated as a separate commercial pillar
- reuse of compliance-family evidence across regulator-facing, brand-facing, or admin-facing
  surfaces without changing family ownership

It does not mean:

- a separate top-level family outside `Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit`
- a replacement for the underlying traceability, certification, or audit evidence family
- a logistics, fulfillment, routing, or commercial access family
- a runtime completeness claim, export redesign, or schema redesign

Current posture:

- DPP is now explicit enough to be treated as a named compliance-family subfamily for later
  bounded reduction
- implementation/design depth remains separately gated by DPP-specific authorities such as
  `docs/architecture/DPP-SNAPSHOT-VIEWS-DESIGN.md`

## 6. What Does Not Belong Inside Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit Scope

The following are adjacent or separate families and must not be silently absorbed into compliance-
family design:

### 6.1 Platform Control-Plane as a whole family

- cross-tenant governance shell, tenant operations, finance oversight, system health, feature
  control, or support tooling as a whole-family substitute
- compliance queue, audit logs, or traceability admin surfaces treated as if they define the full
  family by UI presence alone

### 6.2 B2B Exchange Core as a whole family

- governed trade posture, RFQ, negotiation, supplier/buyer workflow, trade execution, or exchange
  state as a whole-family substitute
- compliance-aware gating or auditable trade progression treated as if they define the whole
  governance-evidence family in full

### 6.3 Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase as a whole downstream family

- order creation, checkout, fulfillment-state, or post-order progression as a whole-family
  substitute
- downstream order evidence or status visibility treated as if it defines the whole family

### 6.4 Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility as a whole downstream family

- finance-state progression, escrow review, settlement administration, or fee visibility as a
  whole-family substitute
- payment or settlement oversight treated as if it defines the full governance-evidence family

### 6.5 Identity, messaging, routing, or other cross-cutting families

- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace continuity as a whole-family substitute
- messaging / notifications as a whole-family substitute
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management as a whole-family substitute

### 6.6 Generic surface presence or implementation work

- audit-log tables, certification tabs, compliance queues, or traceability pages treated as whole-
  family proof merely because they render in a shell
- policy-engine redesign, export redesign, schema changes, API redesign, runtime completion, or
  broader governance-program reinvention

## 7. Relationship To Adjacent Families

### 7.1 Relationship to Platform Control-Plane

- Platform control-plane supervises compliance tasks, disputes, audit visibility, and related
  governance operations.
- Platform control-plane does not define the whole compliance/audit family.
- Governance modules inside the control center are evidence of adjacent reuse, not canonical whole-
  family ownership.

### 7.2 Relationship to B2B Exchange Core

- B2B depends on compliance-aware gating, trust posture, and auditable trade progression.
- Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit does not define the whole B2B family.
- Exchange-family governance language is adjacent evidence, not whole-family authority.

### 7.3 Relationship to B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce

- B2C may later consume bounded certification, traceability, or audit visibility where lawful.
- Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit does not define B2C parent-family storefront
  continuity or consumer-commerce posture.
- B2C remains an adjacent consuming family, not the owner of governance-evidence truth.

### 7.4 Relationship to Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase

- Orders may depend on traceability, evidence continuity, or auditability around downstream state.
- Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit does not define downstream order continuity.
- Order-attached evidence remains downstream reuse, not ownership of the family.

### 7.5 Relationship to Payments / Escrow / Settlement / Fee Visibility

- Payments may depend on reviewed evidence, settlement oversight, or auditability.
- Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit does not define downstream finance-state
  continuity.
- Finance-adjacent audit or oversight remains downstream reuse, not ownership of the family.

### 7.6 Relationship to Identity / Tenancy / Permissions / Workspace Continuity

- Identity/workspace continuity establishes the lawful actor, realm, and tenant boundary through
  which governance evidence is viewed or acted upon.
- Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit does not define identity, permissions, or
  workspace continuity.
- Governance evidence must remain distinct from actor and session continuity.

### 7.7 Relationship to Messaging / Notifications

- Messaging may report compliance events, certification changes, or audit-triggered alerts.
- Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit does not define communication continuity.
- Event reporting must remain distinct from governance-evidence ownership.

### 7.8 Relationship to Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management

- Routing and brand-surface context may determine where governance surfaces are reached.
- Compliance / Certifications / Traceability / Audit does not define tenant resolution, route
  context, or branded surface ownership.
- Access-path continuity remains distinct from governance-evidence continuity.

## 8. Current Sources Of Drift This Family Design Is Meant To Stop

This family design is specifically meant to stop the following recurring planning errors:

- treating control-plane compliance queue, audit logs, or traceability admin surfaces as if the
  control-plane shell owns the whole family
- treating B2B compliance-aware gating and auditable progression as if exchange-family language
  defines the whole governance-evidence boundary
- treating downstream order or finance oversight surfaces as if those downstream families own all
  certification, traceability, and audit truth
- treating launch-readiness inventories or current-state module lists as if enumerated surfaces are
  sufficient family authority
- treating generic audit-log, certification, traceability, or compliance UI presence as whole-
  family proof
- widening this family into policy-engine redesign, export redesign, or runtime delivery claims

## 9. Inheritance Rules For Later Units

Later bounded units that touch compliance, certification, traceability, or audit must inherit the
following rules unless superseded by a tighter authority:

- do not collapse the family into platform control-plane merely because the main operational views
  currently live there
- do not collapse the family into B2B merely because exchange truth requires auditability and
  compliance-aware gating
- do not treat orders or payments evidence as proof that those downstream families own the
  cross-cutting governance-evidence boundary
- do not treat surface presence as sufficient authority without preserving the family-level meaning
  defined here
- keep future work bounded to the specific slice under review rather than widening into broad
  governance-program redesign

## 10. Deferred Or Explicitly Out-Of-Scope Questions

This artifact intentionally does not settle:

- policy engine redesign or broader compliance-program automation
- audit export design, external reporting pipelines, or retention architecture redesign
- certification schema redesign, provenance-model redesign, or broader traceability execution plan
- implementation completeness claims for every surfaced governance module
- cross-family runtime sequencing beyond the narrow current planning boundary
