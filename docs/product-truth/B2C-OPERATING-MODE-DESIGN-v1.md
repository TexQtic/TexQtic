## 1. Purpose and Authority

This artifact is the canonical current family-level design anchor for
`B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce`.

Its purpose is narrow:

- define the lawful B2C family under the adopted canonical model
- separate B2C parent-family truth from narrower storefront-entry continuity, cross-mode
  catalog/discovery ownership, white-label overlay presentation, and downstream orders/checkout
  continuity
- give later family-specific units one reusable B2C boundary so future planning does not keep
  inferring B2C family truth from public shell presence, browse-entry fixes, or branded storefront
  presentation alone

This artifact does not authorize implementation, does not redesign adjacent families, and does not
replace Layer 0 governance truth.

## 2. Current Controlling Posture

Current repo authority already fixes the following truths:

- TexQtic is the operating system for trusted textile supply chains.
- The only governed commercial access models are B2B Exchange, B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce, and
  Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff.
- White-label is an overlay capability and deployment/experience model, not a separate commercial
  access model.
- Enterprise is subordinate depth within B2B, not a separate platform mode.
- Control-plane, superadmin, and WL-admin surfaces are governance/operations surfaces, not
  commercial pillars.
- B2C under current truth is not a fully anonymous public retail model.
- Current repo truth already evidences a real tenant-branded B2C storefront entry seam with
  bounded public-safe discovery plus separate tenant-scoped continuity beyond that seam.

The remaining planning gap is not whether B2C exists. The gap is that current repo truth still
describes B2C mainly through one bounded storefront-entry continuity artifact and adjacent
public-surface correction units rather than through one current explicit family-level design anchor.

That leaves B2C exposed to several drift patterns:

- B2C being reduced to browse-entry continuity only
- catalog/discovery being mistaken for the whole B2C family
- white-label branded presentation being mistaken for the whole B2C family
- downstream cart/checkout/order/post-purchase continuity being mistaken for the whole B2C family
- generic public shell presence being mistaken for lawful family authority
- older wording drifting back toward fully anonymous public retail claims

## 3. Lawful B2C Classification

The controlling classification is:

- B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce is a governed commercial access model
- B2C is a tenant-branded consumer-commerce family
- B2C may expose public-safe discovery and entry surfaces under current truth
- B2C transactional continuity remains tenant-scoped and authenticated unless later board-level
  authority expands it

B2C is therefore not:

- a fully anonymous public retail model under current truth
- a synonym for white-label overlay
- a synonym for catalog/discovery as a whole cross-mode family
- a synonym for downstream orders/checkout/post-purchase continuity
- a synonym for tenant back-office or platform control-plane administration

The correct high-level B2C family statement is:

- B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce is the tenant-branded consumer-commerce family through which public-
  safe discovery and entry connect into tenant-scoped authenticated continuity without turning
  TexQtic into an open-bazaar public retail platform.

## 4. What B2C Means In TexQtic Planning Truth

Within TexQtic planning truth, `B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce` means the parent consumer-commerce
family through which a tenant presents a branded storefront or consumer-facing commerce posture
while keeping governed continuity aligned to tenant-scoped identity, permissions, and operational
truth.

At the family level, this means:

- a tenant-branded consumer storefront or consumer entry posture exists as a lawful parent-family
  surface
- public-safe discovery and browse-entry may exist where repo truth supports them
- once the lawful public-safe boundary is crossed, consumer-commerce continuity remains tenant-
  scoped and authenticated under current truth
- branded storefront/product presentation belongs to B2C parent-family truth without absorbing the
  whole catalog/discovery family that supports it
- downstream commerce continuity such as cart, checkout, orders, and post-purchase may belong to
  later connected families without being silently absorbed into B2C family definition here

## 5. What Belongs Inside B2C Scope

The following belong inside the B2C family boundary:

### 5.1 Tenant-branded consumer storefront posture

- tenant-branded consumer storefront framing
- branded consumer-facing entry posture for lawful B2C tenants
- consumer-facing storefront continuity at the parent-family level where the purpose is to present
  a lawful tenant-branded commerce surface rather than to define a narrow browse-only seam

### 5.2 Public-safe discovery and entry

- public-safe browse-entry continuity where repo truth already supports it
- bounded public-facing product discovery or preview continuity appropriate to current B2C truth
- public-safe consumer entry behavior that does not overclaim anonymous full-retail depth

### 5.3 Tenant-scoped authenticated continuity after entry

- authenticated continuity for consumer-commerce activity after the public-safe boundary is crossed
- tenant-scoped continuity for identity-bound or user-bound commerce behavior under current truth
- the family-level distinction between public-safe entry and authenticated downstream continuity

### 5.4 B2C parent-family storefront/product presentation

- branded product presentation and storefront-facing consumer experience as part of B2C parent
  truth
- use of shared catalog/product data inside a B2C parent-family context without claiming the whole
  catalog family as B2C-owned
- family-level consumer-commerce framing that later downstream families can inherit without
  collapsing B2C into shell presence or one exact storefront seam

## 6. What Does Not Belong Inside B2C Scope

The following are adjacent or later families and must not be silently absorbed into B2C family
design:

### 6.1 Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity as a whole family

- cross-mode catalog/discovery/product-data ownership in full
- search, list, detail, preview, and product-data continuity as a whole-family substitute for B2C
- catalog-backed browse preview treated as if it defines the whole B2C family

### 6.2 White-Label Overlay as a whole family

- branded overlay/runtime presentation as a whole-family substitute
- WL admin/operator or domain-owned overlay behavior treated as if it defines B2C family truth
- any attempt to make white-label the canonical owner of B2C identity

### 6.3 Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase as a whole family

- cart, checkout, order lifecycle, and post-purchase continuity as a whole downstream family
- any attempt to define the whole B2C family through later transaction-execution seams

### 6.4 Payments / Escrow / Settlement as a whole family

- payment-state, settlement-state, or finance-event continuity as a separate downstream family
- any attempt to treat payment lifecycle as the canonical definition of B2C family scope

### 6.5 Tenant Back Office as a whole family

- tenant-owned org administration, memberships, settings, branding administration, and
  integrations
- seller/admin or tenant-operator controls treated as if they define B2C family truth

### 6.6 Platform Control-Plane as a whole family

- cross-tenant supervision, governance, audit, feature controls, or platform operations
- platform visibility into B2C surfaces treated as if it defines the B2C family

### 6.7 B2B Exchange Core and Aggregator as whole families

- B2B governed business exchange continuity in full
- Aggregator directory discovery and intent handoff continuity in full
- any attempt to define B2C as a consumerized variant of B2B or Aggregator family truth

### 6.8 Identity, domain, and implementation families

- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace continuity as a whole family
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management as a whole family
- implementation work, runtime completion claims, schema changes, or broad redesign work

## 7. Relationship To Adjacent Families

### 7.1 Relationship to Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity

- B2C uses catalog/discovery/product-data continuity for browse, preview, and product inspection.
- That supporting family does not define the whole B2C parent family.
- B2C must not be reduced to catalog-backed browse preview alone.

### 7.2 Relationship to White-Label Overlay

- White-label may overlay B2C where lawful.
- White-label does not define B2C family truth and does not replace B2C as the parent commercial
  access model.
- Public non-WL B2C storefront truth and broader B2C family truth remain separate from WL overlay
  normalization.

### 7.3 Relationship to Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase

- B2C may connect into downstream cart, checkout, order, and post-purchase continuity.
- Those downstream families remain separately refinable and must not be silently absorbed into B2C
  family scope here.
- B2C defines the parent consumer-commerce context, not the complete downstream transaction family.

### 7.4 Relationship to Tenant Back Office

- Tenant back office may support tenant-owned B2C operator administration in bounded form.
- Tenant admin/operator controls do not define the whole B2C family.
- Public storefront seams must not be read as evidence of seller/admin ownership on the same seam
  unless a later unit explicitly proves it.

### 7.5 Relationship to Platform Control-Plane

- Platform control-plane may supervise or govern B2C-facing platform posture in bounded form.
- Control-plane does not define the whole B2C family or broader storefront truth.

### 7.6 Relationship to B2B Exchange Core

- B2B and B2C are distinct governed commercial access models.
- B2C must not be collapsed into authenticated business exchange posture.
- B2B catalog/commercial continuity and B2C consumer-commerce continuity remain distinct parent
  families even where they share supporting catalog or downstream transaction families.

### 7.7 Relationship to Aggregator Directory Discovery + Intent Handoff

- Aggregator remains a separate discovery-and-handoff commercial access model.
- B2C consumer storefront discovery must not be reinterpreted as Aggregator directory ownership.

### 7.8 Relationship to Identity / Tenancy / Permissions / Workspace Continuity

- authenticated B2C continuity depends on identity, tenancy, and workspace rules.
- that dependency does not authorize absorbing the full identity/workspace family into B2C here.

### 7.9 Relationship to Domain / Tenant Routing / Brand-Surface Management

- B2C storefront entry and branded surfaces overlap with domain/routing concerns.
- that overlap does not authorize solving the full domain/routing/brand-surface family here.

## 8. Current Sources Of Drift

The main current drift classes are:

- the existing B2C launch continuity artifact is accurate but intentionally limited to public-safe
  browse-entry continuity, which makes it easy to misread as the whole B2C family anchor
- the closed B2C storefront continuity and public-affordance separation units are runtime-boundary
  corrections only, but their existence can make the exact public seam look like the whole family
- catalog-backed browse preview can be mistaken for the whole B2C family because it is the most
  explicit current public B2C truth in repo artifacts
- white-label storefront/runtime language can make branded storefront behavior look like the whole
  B2C family if parent/overlay classification is not restated
- generic public storefront shell presence can be mistaken for lawful family authority even when it
  proves only one narrow public seam
- older marketplace or full-retail shorthand can drift B2C back toward anonymous public retail
  overstatement

These sources remain useful evidence of runtime or bounded planning truth, but they must not serve
as the canonical family-classification authority going forward.

## 9. Inheritance Rules For Later Family Units

Later family-specific units must inherit all of the following:

1. B2C Tenant-Branded Commerce is a distinct governed commercial access model.
2. B2C is not a fully anonymous public retail model under current truth.
3. Public-safe discovery and entry may exist in B2C, but transactional continuity remains tenant-
   scoped and authenticated unless later board-level authority expands it.
4. Catalog / Discovery / Product Data Continuity may support B2C, but does not define the whole
   B2C family.
5. White-label may overlay B2C where lawful, but WL does not define B2C family truth.
6. Orders / Checkout / Post-Purchase, Payments / Escrow / Settlement, Identity / Workspace, and
   Domain / Routing families remain separate later families.
7. Tenant Back Office and Platform Control-Plane remain separate administrative families.
8. B2C must not be treated as evidence of open-bazaar or undifferentiated public marketplace
   behavior.
9. Public shell presence or one exact storefront seam must not be used as proof of the whole B2C
   family definition.
10. This family boundary does not imply implementation completeness.

## 10. Deferred To Later Units

This family anchor deliberately does not solve:

- orders / checkout / post-purchase family design
- payments / escrow / settlement family design
- identity / tenancy / permissions / workspace family design
- domain / tenant routing / brand-surface management family design
- broader subscription / entitlements / packaging decisions
- implementation planning or runtime changes

Those remain later bounded units.

Result of this artifact:
