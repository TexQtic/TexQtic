## Why Conditional Elevation Does Not Equal Product Opening

Conditional elevation does not equal product opening because these are different authority moves.

Conditional elevation only means that a family-unit artifact could later be recognized as bounded
topic-specific downstream authority under the April reset baseline.

It does not mean:

1. the artifact becomes the repo-level next-step selector
2. a product-facing next opening is chosen
3. implementation is authorized
4. zero-open posture is suspended
5. Layer 0 is bypassed

This distinction matters most for the artifacts reviewed here:

1. eligible B2B and B2C anchors may later help bound family-specific reasoning, but they cannot
   choose the next delivery opening
2. the eligible taxonomy-propagation map may later help prevent taxonomy drift, but it cannot
   authorize execution or reclassify Aggregator ownership
3. strong Subscription artifacts remain outside family identity and therefore do not convert
   commercial-axis maturity into family-opening authority
